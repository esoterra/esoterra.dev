I talk a lot about Rust and WebAssembly (Wasm). Maybe these sound a lot like shiny new things, and they are but that's only one piece of the story. What else do they have in common?

Core promises

Rust and Wasm share a lot of the same core promises, ones they deliver on.

They are both Secure. Rust's memory model addresses a large class of common vulnerabilities and Wasm's capability-orientation and strong validation/isolation offer new opportunities for security.

They are both Performant. Rust's systems-level design and strictness allow advanced user and compiler optimizations. Wasm's low-level stack machine instruction set and strong typing let it compile to efficient machine code on virtually all machines.

They are both Portable. Rust compiles well to commonly supported platforms and its standard library does a good job of abstracting platform specifics where possible. Wasm is king of platform independence, its semantics are well-defined and any compliant embedding will behave the same; under some constraints even deterministically!

Those are some cool features! I like them and I think they're well suited to the requirements of many of today's application's requirements. But what if I told you that they're not the real reason I love these technologies?

The Real Reason

More fundamentally than security, performance, and portability, these technologies make sense and free us from many legacies. They offer us a future where we have the tools to better manage the complexity of our field.

They are new and some people might say they've been taking forever to get off the ground, but that's because they've learned a ton from what came before, and spent their time making the best decisions they could. They stand on the shoulder's of giants and decades of experience, and they made the most of that.

I'm not trying to insult any predecessors and I don't want to imply that newer = better; there are plenty of counter-examples. Not every new technology will be shinier and/or better than the last, but sometimes things that come along that are unique opportunities and if we can protect them they can become something incredible.

Enough waxing poetic, let's talk about the sensible future these technologies enable and why it's so worth pursuing.
